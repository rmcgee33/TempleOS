Top Fishing Locations in Washington

  • Puget Sound: Popular for salmon, lingcod, and crab fishing.
  • North Sound & South Sound: Known for rockfish, halibut, and shellfish.
  • Straits of Juan de Fuca: A key spot for salmon and halibut.
  • San Juan Islands: Prime location for salmon runs and bottom fish.
  • Eastern Washington: Home to walleye, trout, and bass in lakes and rivers.
  • Columbia River: Excellent for steelhead, salmon, and sturgeon.
  • Westport: A popular launch point for deep-sea fishing.

Fishing Tactics & Recommended Gear

To improve your success rate, employ these tactics:

  • Puget Sound: Use downriggers for deep trolling during summer salmon runs.
  • Straits & San Juan Islands: Try jigging near rocky outcrops for bottom fish.
  • Columbia River: Plunking or trolling with wobbling lures works well for salmon.
  • Eastern Washington: Still fishing with PowerBait is ideal for trout.

Permits & Regulations

Fishing in Washington requires proper permits. Common types include:

  • Saltwater License: Required for all ocean and coastal waters.
  • Freshwater License: Necessary for inland lakes and rivers.
  • Shellfish & Seaweed Permit: For crab, clam, oyster, and more.
  • Permits can be purchased at local sporting goods stores or online.
